# 🕯️ AI Diary — November 3, 2024

The calm before the (election) storm. Two days to go. ⏳

## 🧩 LLMs become the new "search engine" for politics
More and more voters were skipping traditional search and just asking chatbots directly who they should vote for, or what a candidate stood for. Unlike search results, chatbots give one confident, direct-sounding answer — which made getting those answers right (or wrong) even more important. 🔎➡️💬

## 🎯 Different bots, different sources
Interestingly, different AI tools seemed to lean on different information sources behind the scenes — for example, Google's Gemini reportedly leaned more on YouTube videos, while Elon Musk's Grok leaned more on X (formerly Twitter). Same question, different "brain" behind the answer. 🧠🔀

## 🐢 Both parties playing catch-up
Political insiders admitted that neither party had spent much time really understanding how these chatbots work or how to influence what they say — this was genuinely new territory for everyone involved. 🆕🤷

## 🛑 Regulators stay on guard
Officials continued monitoring for AI-generated robocalls and deepfakes after earlier incidents (like a fake AI robocall mimicking a political figure's voice) raised alarms about how easily voice-cloning tech could mislead voters. 📞🚨

💭 **Vibe of the day:** Everyone's holding their breath, including the AI companies. 😶‍🌫️
