# 📈 AI Diary — November 6, 2024

The election results are in — and somehow, AI is right in the middle of the story. 🤖🇺🇸

## 🏆 Nvidia becomes the world's most valuable company, again
On the very day Donald Trump's election victory was confirmed, AI-chip giant Nvidia leapfrogged Apple to become the most valuable company on the planet — riding the same generative-AI boom that had also sent Apple, Google, Meta, and Microsoft to record valuations. 💹🔥

## 🔮 What comes next for AI policy?
With a new administration on the way, analysts immediately started speculating about what might change:
- A likely shift away from the Biden administration's more cautious, regulation-heavy approach to AI 🔄
- Renewed focus on competing with China on AI and chip technology 🇨🇳⚔️
- An uncertain future for the US AI Safety Institute, the agency set up to test and evaluate powerful AI models 🏛️❓
- Possible big questions for "open" AI models (like Meta's Llama) given concerns about how freely available AI could be used by rivals 🔓🌍

## 🤝 Friends in high places
Tech watchers also pointed out that some of AI's biggest names — including Elon Musk and his AI company xAI — had grown notably close to the incoming administration, meaning the AI industry's relationship with Washington was about to look very different. 🤝🏛️

## 🔁 Echoes of 2016... but different this time
Back in 2016, AI hype quietly faded into the background after a similarly surprising election result. This time, though, most analysts agreed AI had become simply too big, too valuable, and too central to the economy to fade from the spotlight again. 🔄🚫
