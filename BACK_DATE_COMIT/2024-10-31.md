# 🎃 AI Diary — October 31, 2024

Spooky season wraps up, and so does a genuinely massive month for AI. Let's look back! 👻📅

## 🏅 AI wins its first-ever Nobel Prizes
Earlier this month, the Nobel committee handed out two prizes connected to AI for the very first time:
- **Physics** went to John Hopfield and Geoffrey Hinton for foundational work on neural networks — the math behind how AI "learns." 🧠⚡
- **Chemistry** went to Demis Hassabis, John Jumper, and David Baker for AlphaFold, the AI system that predicts the 3D shapes of proteins — something scientists had struggled with for decades. 🧬🏆

It was a historic moment: AI research being recognized at the very top of science. 🎉

## 🛡️ Biden signs the first AI national security memo
The White House released its first-ever AI-focused National Security Memorandum, directing US agencies to stay ahead in AI development while protecting human rights and strengthening chip supply chains. 🇺🇸📜

## 👋 Another OpenAI safety team disbands
Miles Brundage, OpenAI's senior advisor on "AGI Readiness," announced he was leaving the company — and that his team was being disbanded. It followed several other high-profile safety-focused departures earlier in the year. 🚪😕

## 📊 Chatbots still struggle with the truth
A monthly misinformation report found that the top 10 AI chatbots collectively repeated false claims in roughly a quarter of their answers when tested on hot-button news topics — a number that actually went up from the month before. 🤥📈

---
💭 **Vibe of the day:** AI got a Nobel Prize and a safety-team shake-up in the same month. Spooky indeed. 🎃🤖
