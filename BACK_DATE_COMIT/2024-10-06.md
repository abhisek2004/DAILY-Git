# 🎞️ AI Diary — October 6, 2024

Still riding the wave from Meta's big AI video reveal earlier this week. 🌊

## 🎭 Hollywood starts paying attention
As more details about Movie Gen spread, people in the entertainment industry started raising eyebrows. AI video generators like this one (and OpenAI's Sora) had already made some filmmakers nervous about what realistic AI-made footage could mean for the movie business. 🎬😬

## 🏷️ Meta promises to label AI content
To address some of those worries, Meta said any AI-manipulated content made with its tools would be tagged "Made with AI" across its apps — an attempt to keep people from being fooled by content that looks real but isn't. 🏷️🔍

## 📱 Coming soon to your phone
Meta confirmed Movie Gen wasn't fully public yet — only employees and select partners had access for now — but it's expected to eventually power fun features in Instagram Reels and personalized WhatsApp greetings. 💬🎉

## 🥽 Echoes of Meta's other AI reveal
This all followed close on the heels of Meta showing off its Orion AR glasses prototype, which also leaned on AI for things like 3D visualization and spatial recognition — a reminder that Meta's betting big on AI across hardware *and* software. 🕶️🤖

💭 **Vibe of the day:** Cool tech, but also: "wait, can we trust what we're watching anymore?" 🤔📺
