# 📊 AI Diary — November 2, 2024

Weekend before the big election, and AI is quietly becoming part of how people get political info. 🗳️

## 🛰️ Campaigns are watching what AI says about them
With millions of people now asking chatbots like ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, and Claude for answers, political campaigns realized something: the answers these bots give about candidates actually matter. Analytics officers from the Harris campaign had built a tool called **Caucus AI** just to track and log what different chatbots were saying about candidates state by state. 🕵️‍♀️📋

## 🤷 Nobody's totally sure how much influence AI chatbots have
One campaign strategist put it well: AI chatbots are probably going to be more politically influential than AI skeptics think, but less influential than AI enthusiasts think — and either way, that influence is only growing. ⚖️

## 📚 AI helping (not hurting) some voters
On a more hopeful note, researchers had been testing a custom chatbot called **BallotBot**, built specifically to help California voters understand confusing ballot measures. Early results showed it made people feel like understanding the issues took less effort — especially for voters who started out less informed. 🌟📖

---
💭 **Vibe of the day:** AI: equal parts election headache and election helper. 🤖⚖️
