# 🗳️ AI Diary — November 1, 2024

Four days until the US presidential election, and AI chatbots are suddenly under a spotlight. 🔍

## ⚠️ "Don't trust the chatbot with your voting questions"

New York's Attorney General put out a public warning today: AI chatbots were frequently giving **inaccurate** answers when tested with basic voting questions, like where and how to vote. The office said relying on them could mean voters "lose their opportunity to vote" because of bad info. 😬🗳️

## 🤖 AI companies respond

- **Anthropic** said it agreed people should stick to official sources for voting info, since Claude isn't updated frequently enough to give reliable real-time election details. ✅
- **OpenAI** confirmed that starting November 5th (election day!), ChatGPT would start nudging people who ask about results toward trusted sources like the Associated Press and Reuters, or their local election board. 📰

## 🕵️ Fighting fake campaigns

OpenAI also revealed it had shut down more than 20 shady operations that tried to misuse its models for things like fake news articles and social media bot posts — though it said none of them gained much traction. 🚫🤖

## 🎭 Deepfakes on the rise

Separately, a machine-learning firm reported that the number of deepfakes had jumped 900% compared to last year — including some tied to attempts to mess with the US election. 📈😬

---

💭 **Vibe of the day:** AI's getting a crash course in "with great power comes great responsibility." 🕸️
